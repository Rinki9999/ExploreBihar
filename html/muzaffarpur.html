Explore Muzaffarpur

Why Visit Muzaffarpur?

Muzaffarpur is popularly known as the "Land of Shahi Litchi", exporting its delicious fruit across the globe. Beyond litchis, the city holds historical, cultural, and natural significance, making it a wonderful destination for travelers seeking a mix of taste and tradition.

Best Time to Visit

May to July: Litchi season, best for fruit lovers.
October to March: Pleasant weather for sightseeing and festivals.

Top Places to Visit in Muzaffarpur

  • Litchi Gardens: Visit orchards during the harvest season for fresh, juicy litchis and nature photography.
  • Baba Garib Nath Temple: A revered Shiva temple in the heart of the city, attracting thousands of devotees.
  • Ram Chandra Shahi Museum: A colonial-era museum with rare art pieces, coins, and historical artifacts.
  • Katra Garh Fort: A hidden historical site perfect for exploring the city's past.
  • Jubba Sahni Park: A peaceful retreat named after a freedom fighter, ideal for families and kids.

Why Muzaffarpur's Litchi is Famous

Muzaffarpur's Shahi Litchi is renowned for its juicy sweetness, rosy skin, and unique flavor. It holds a GI (Geographical Indication) tag and is a symbol of the region's pride and economy.

Litchi Orchard During Harvest
One can visit the local litchi orchards for an unforgettable experience of picking the freshest litchis straight from the trees.
Baba Garib Nath Temple
Garib Sthan Mandir is a highly revered shrine located in the heart of Muzaffarpur. This sacred site is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is known for its rich history and spiritual ambience. The temple attracts devotees from all around the region, especially during the month of Sawan, when a special fair is held, and the Shivratri festival, celebrated with great enthusiasm.
Ram Chandra Shahi Museum
this museum is named after the late Kshatriya Rajput chief who was instrumental in its foundation. It provides a glimpse into the region's past with a collection that includes archaeological artifacts, sculptures, terracotta items, and items of historical art. The museum's architecture is a sight in itself, reflecting traditional Indian styles. The exhibits trace back to the early centuries of Indian history, providing a window into the lives and times of various Indian civilizations that influenced the region.
Jubba Sahni Park
The park is named after Jubba Sahni, a freedom fighter and social worker who played an important role in India's independence movement. This park, which was inaugurated in his memory in 1985, covers a sprawling area and features several amenities.
