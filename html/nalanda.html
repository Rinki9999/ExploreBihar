Why Visit Nalanda?

Nalanda is one of the world‚Äôs oldest centers of learning, home to the famous Nalanda University. A UNESCO World Heritage Site, it offers a deep dive into India's Buddhist history, architecture, and spiritual legacy.
Nalanda Mahavihara, a renowned Buddhist monastery and learning center, was established in the 5th century CE by Kumaragupta I of the Gupta dynasty. It flourished as a major educational institution for over 800 years, attracting students and scholars from across Asia. Nalanda was a center of learning for various subjects, including philosophy, logic, grammar, and medicine

Best Time to Visit

November to March: Cool and pleasant weather for exploring the ruins and museums.

Top Places to Visit in Nalanda

  • Nalanda University Ruins: Ancient learning center with monasteries, lecture halls, and student quarters.
  • Nalanda Archaeological Museum: Preserves sculptures, artifacts, and inscriptions from the ancient university.
  • Xuanzang Memorial Hall: Built in memory of the Chinese scholar who studied here in the 7th century.
  • Surya Mandir: An old sun temple near Nalanda ruins.
  • Nava Nalanda Mahavihara: A modern research university built in the ancient tradition of Nalanda.

Legacy of Nalanda

Nalanda was a beacon of knowledge and Buddhist learning in ancient India, attracting scholars from China, Korea, Tibet, and beyond. Its architectural brilliance and academic excellence made it legendary.

Nalanda University Ruins
It flourished for over 800 years, attracting scholars from across Asia, and was renowned for its excellence in various disciplines, including Buddhism, astronomy, medicine, logic, and mathematics.
Nava Nalanda Mahavihara
Nalanda was one of the world's first residential universities. More than 10,000 students and 2,000 teachers resided in this monastic campus. The Mahavihara, built in red bricks, was considered to be an architectural masterpiece.
Xuanzang Memorial Hall
Xuan Zang memorial hall is dedicated to a Buddhist monk scholar who travelled from China to India in the Seventh Century. The memorial hall is built in line with Chinees architecture and situated at a calm place. The entire premises including memorial hall, garden, pathways are neatly maintained.
Nalanda Museum
Nalanda Museum is one of the most prestigious site-museums of Archaeological Survey of India and houses antiquities found at the site of Nalanda Mahavihara. Nalanda Museum established in the year 1917 is one of the most prestigious site-museums of Archaeological Survey of India.
