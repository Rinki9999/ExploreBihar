Explore Pawapuri

Why Visit Pawapuri?

Pawapuri, located in Bihar, is a sacred Jain site where Lord Mahavira attained Nirvana and was cremated around 500 BCE. The site is famous for the Jal Mandir, a stunning white marble temple built in the middle of a lotus-filled pond. It also houses the Samosharan Temple, marking the place of Mahavira’s final sermon. Surrounded by nature and peace, Pawapuri is a serene destination for spiritual seekers and history lovers alike.

Best Time to Visit

October to March: Cool and peaceful—perfect for temple visits and exploring Jain festivals.

Top Places to Visit in Pawapuri

  • Jal Mandir: A stunning marble temple in the middle of a lotus pond, marking the cremation site of Mahavira.
  • Samosharan Temple:Built where Mahavira preached after attaining omniscience.
  • Gaon Mandir: A peaceful temple near the town entrance surrounded by greenery.

How to Reach Pawapuri

  • By Train: Nearest railway stations are Bihar Sharif and Rajgir.
  • By Road: 90 km from Patna; taxis and buses available.
  • Nearest Airport: Gaya Airport or Patna Airport.

Peaceful Vibes of Pawapuri

Pawapuri is a place where time slows down and spirituality takes over. With sacred ponds, white marble temples, and the aura of Mahavira's teachings, it’s an ideal destination for those seeking inner peace.

Jal Mandir in Lotus Pond
The Jal Mandir in Pawapuri is a prominent Jain temple dedicated to Lord Mahavira, the 24th Tirthankara, and is located in a tank filled with red lotuses. It marks the site of his cremation and is considered a significant pilgrimage site for Jains. The temple enshrines the "Charan Paduka," or foot impression, of Lord Mahavira, and is believed to have been built by his brother, King Nandivardhan.
