Explore Rajgir

Why Visit Rajgir?

Rajgir, nestled in the hills of Bihar, is a historical city known for its connection to both Buddhism and Jainism. It was the first capital of the Magadh kingdom and holds significance due to Buddha and Mahavira’s presence. The natural beauty, ropeway rides, ancient ruins, and hot springs make Rajgir a perfect spiritual and nature retreat.

Best Time to Visit

October to March: Ideal for sightseeing, nature walks, and ropeway rides. Rajgir Mahotsav also takes place in December.

Top Places to Visit in Rajgir

  • Vishwa Shanti Stupa: A beautiful white peace pagoda atop Ratnagiri hill, reached by ropeway.
  • Venu Van: Bamboo grove gifted to Lord Buddha by King Bimbisara for meditation.
  • Hot Springs: Sacred hot water springs believed to have healing properties, especially Brahmakund.
  • Ajatshatru Fort: Ruins of a 6th-century BC fort built by King Ajatshatru of Magadh.
  • Saptaparni Cave: Where the first Buddhist council was held after Buddha’s Mahaparinirvana.

Rajgir’s Beauty & Spiritual Calm

Rajgir offers a unique blend of history, peace, and natural beauty – from the ancient ruins to serene hills and healing hot springs, it’s a perfect destination for heritage lovers and spiritual seekers.

Vishwa Shanti Stupa - Peace Pagoda
The Vishwa Shanti Stupa, or Peace Pagoda, is a symbol of peace and love, erected to disseminate love and peace and create "The pure Land" on earth. It was initiated by Nichidatsu Fuji Guruji, after seeing the tragedy of the atomic bombs in Hiroshima and Nagasaki.
Ropeway to Shanti Stupa
The Vishwa Shanti Stupa in Rajgir, Bihar, is accessible by a ropeway, also known as a chairlift, which was gifted by Japanese Buddhist monk Nichidatsu Fujii in the 1960s. The ropeway offers a convenient way to reach the stupa located atop Ratnagiri Hill, making it accessible to a wider range of visitors.
Brahmakund Hot Water Spring
Brahmakund in Rajgir, Bihar, is a renowned hot spring believed to have healing properties and sacred significance for Hindus, Buddhists, and Jains. It's one of several hot springs in the area, and its water, collected in a large pool, is considered especially sacred and believed to help with various ailments.
Saptaparni Cave - 1st Buddhist Council
The Saptaparni Cave in Rajgir, Bihar, is a significant site in Buddhist history as it's believed to be the location of the First Buddhist Council. This council convened roughly three months after the Buddha's death, and it was held under the patronage of King Ajatashatru. The council's primary purpose was to preserve and transmit Buddha's teachings to future generations.
