Best Time to Visit

October to March: Pleasant weather and higher chances of spotting wildlife make this the perfect time for a jungle adventure and nature walks.

Top Attractions in Valmikinagar

  • Valmiki Tiger Reserve: The pride of Bihar, rich in tigers, leopards, deer, and rare birds.
  • Gandak River: Beautiful riverside spots perfect for relaxation and nature photography.
  • Valmiki Ashram: Believed to be the site where Maharishi Valmiki wrote the Ramayana.
  • Tharu Village: Cultural experience with the indigenous Tharu community and their traditions.

How to Reach Valmikinagar

  • By Train: Nearest station is Valmikinagar Road (VMKR), connected via Narkatiaganj.
  • By Road: Around 270 km from Patna and 90 km from Bettiah. Buses and taxis available.
  • Nearest Airport: Patna Airport or Gorakhpur Airport.

Into the Wild

Valmikinagar offers an unforgettable blend of wilderness and peace. From tigers in their natural habitat to peaceful ashram visits, it's an enriching escape from city life.

Valmiki Tiger Reserve
Valmiki Tiger Reserve, located in the northwestern part of Bihar near the India-Nepal border, is Bihar's only tiger reserve. It encompasses the Valmiki National Park and the Valmiki Wildlife Sanctuary, covering approximately 880 sq km. The reserve is situated in the Gangetic Plains bio-geographic zone and forms the easternmost limit of the Himalayan Terai forests in India.
Riverside Serenity
"Riverside Serenity" is a common phrase describing a state of peace and tranquility found by a river or stream. It can be used to describe a place, a feeling, or even a lifestyle. The phrase evokes images of quietness, natural beauty, and a sense of calm and relaxation.
Ancient Valmiki Ashram
Valmiki Ashram is a significant location in Hindu mythology, believed to be the place where the sage Valmiki composed the epic Ramayana. It is also associated with Sita, Rama's wife, who is said to have sought refuge there during her pregnancy and gave birth to their sons, Luv and Kush, within the ashram.
