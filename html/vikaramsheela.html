Explore Vikramsheela

Why Visit Vikramsheela?

Vikramsheela was one of the most important Buddhist learning centers in ancient India, established by King Dharmapala in the 8th century. Its ruins still reflect the architectural brilliance and spiritual aura of that era.
There was one university , The university had around 100 teachers and over 1,000 students, with grand structures like monasteries, prayer halls, lecture halls, and a central stupa. It was destroyed in the 12th century during the invasions of Bakhtiyar Khilji.

Best Time to Visit

November to February: Pleasant weather for exploring the ancient ruins and enjoying the cultural heritage of the site.

Top Attractions in Vikramsheela

  • Vikramsheela Mahavihara: The ruins of a massive Buddhist university spread across several acres.
  • Main Stupa: A large central stupa that is the heart of the ancient monastic complex.
  • Excavation Site: Ongoing research reveals more about ancient Buddhist learning and architecture.

How to Reach Vikramsheela

  • By Train: Nearest station is Kahalgaon, around 13 km away.
  • By Road: Well connected from Bhagalpur (~38 km) and Patna (~300 km).
  • Nearest Airport: Patna or Gaya Airport.

Legacy of Buddhist Education

Vikramsheela was a hub of Buddhist scholarship, second only to Nalanda. Walking among its ruins is like stepping back into the 8th century, when monks from around the world studied and meditated here.

Vikramsheela Mahavihara Ruins
Vikramshila Mahavihara was a renowned center of Buddhist learning in ancient India, established by King Dharmapala of the Pala dynasty. Located near Antichak in Bihar, it was a major university attracting students from across Asia. The site was eventually destroyed by the invading forces of Bakhtiyar Khilji around 1200.
Main Central Stupa
The Main Central Stupa, also known as the Great Stupa at Sanchi, is a significant Buddhist monument in India. It is a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the oldest stone structures in the country.
